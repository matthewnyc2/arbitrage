What this page does, in plain English

Polymarket is a prediction market — people bet real money on real-world questions like "Who wins the 2028 election?" or "Will the Fed cut rates in April?". Every possible answer trades as a share that pays $1 if it wins and $0 if it loses. Because exactly one answer can win, the total price of one share of every answer has to add up to $1 at fair value. When it briefly adds up to less than $1, you can buy one of each and lock in the gap as guaranteed profit. That's the arbitrage.

This page opens a live connection to Polymarket's order book, watches 6 real events, and shows the total cost in real time. If the total crosses below $1, you'll see it turn green in the opportunities panel on the right.

arbitrage alerts

None yet — and that's normal.
Arbitrage windows on Polymarket are rare and brief. Professional trading bots compete them away in milliseconds. You might watch for many minutes before one appears, if ever. That's the honest answer, and seeing it prove out live is more valuable than a fake demo that lights up every second.

what would happen if one fired?

In the full Python version of this bot, a real arbitrage alert kicks off a risk check (basket size, daily loss cap, kill switch), then signs and submits buy orders on every outcome in parallel. Once it holds one share of every answer, it redeems the set for the guaranteed $1 payout and books the spread as profit.

This page never does any of that. It's read-only — it watches, it shows the math, it logs what it sees. No wallet, no orders, no risk.

why you see "no arbitrage" almost always

The math is public, so every serious market-maker watches the same numbers. When the total drops below $1, they pile in within milliseconds and push it back to $1. Retail traders see the occasional scrap on thin, ignored markets — but the obvious opportunities are taken before you can click.
